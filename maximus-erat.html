Behind the Resume

I want you to take a second to see beyond my resume—beyond the array of internships, personal projects, and co-ops. Behind this hard work there is resolute individual; one with a strong-sense of integrity and character committed to the continuous improvement of themself, their community, and the world they live in.

I abide by a philosophy that there is always more improvement to be made. I live by this philosophy, applying it to my primary sphreres of impact: myself, my community, and my profession. At any point in my life for the last 10 years, I have sustained ongoing improvement projects in each sphere, and I have listed the notable ones for each sphere below.

My most notable recent improvements of self involve coding and designing this personal website; numerous self-directed projects in my github—found on the “Github Portfolio” page; a successful small-scale custom sticker and laser printing design business called Powers Designs—found on the “Powers Designs” page of this website; learning to solve a Rubik’s cube at 22 years of age; an emerging homemade hot sauce business I aim to expand to the Farmer’s Market in the Spring; and the successful completion of the 52 Book Challenge for 2023 a month ahead schedule.

A recent notable improvement to my community includes a rise to Presidential leadership within oSTEM@Duke. oSTEM is the largest chapter-based organization focused on uniting, uplifting, and supporting LGBTQ+ individuals in STEM. As President of the Duke chapter in oSTEM, I lead the chapter to foster, form, and empower the community. Another of my improvements to the community includes regular blood donations every 56-60 days over the past two years, aligning with national guidelines to address the persistent blood shortage.

However, I am most proud of my community-centered commitment to the improvement of education. The need for information crosses all borders, and I am committed to having an active role in sharing as much information as I can. For this reason, I am currently a regular substitute teacher at Raleigh Charter High School in math, science, english, and history. I have also been a Teaching Assistant for more than 20 courses in my college career. In fact, I hold the record for the most Teaching Assistantships of any undergraduate or graduate student in the math department, and this does not include the two semesters I spent holding drop-in office hours each week in the Math Help room to serve all students taking any of the 8 introductory math courses.

Lastly, I am focused on improving my professional career. To see my recent notable professional improvements, I encourage you to look at the “Resume” page of this website as well as my github profile. I especially encourage you to look at the letters of recommendation found on the “Letters of Outstanding Improvement” page of this website.

While my professional history is great, so are the professional histories of many aspiring professionals. What truly sets me apart is how I bring my resolute drive, grit, and integrity to every sphere of influence I am a part of. This is important because your work is not just about a profession: your work affects yourself, your communities, and the world you live in. To thrive professionally, communally, and personally, one must be authentically themselves in every aspect of their lives. While you can clearly see in my resume and personal projects that I have achieved high, I hope you will see that I always aim to achieve higher.

Note: I use the word “improvements” instead of “achievements” because achievement implies the work is finished, and I believe that you can always do more, do better, and reach higher.
